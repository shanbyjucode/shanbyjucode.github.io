Leg Adduction


Setup

  • Adjust the leg adduction machine so that the pads rest on the inside of your thighs.
  • Sit upright with your back firmly against the backrest and grip the handles at your sides.
  • Place your feet flat on the footrests and position your thighs against the inner pads.
  • Select an appropriate weight and ensure youâ€™re comfortable before beginning the exercise.

Execution

  • Contract Inward: Squeeze your thighs together by contracting your inner thigh muscles.
  • Controlled Return: Slowly allow your legs to return to the starting position without letting the weights slam down.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Control the Movement: Avoid letting the weights drop quickly; focus on a slow return phase.
  • Full Range: Move through the complete range of motion for maximum activation.
  • Keep Your Back Supported: Do not lean forward; maintain contact with the backrest at all times.
  • Engage Core: Keep your core tight to avoid shifting your torso during the exercise.

Benefits of Leg Adduction

  • Targets Inner Thighs: Isolates and strengthens the adductor muscles of the thighs.
  • Stabilizes Lower Body: Supports balance and movement control in lower body exercises.
  • Improves Hip Stability: Helps in injury prevention and hip joint alignment.
  • Aesthetic Benefits: Helps tone and shape the inner thigh area.
