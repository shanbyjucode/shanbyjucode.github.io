Single Arm Triceps Extension


Setup

  • Stand or sit upright with a dumbbell in one hand.
  • Raise the dumbbell overhead with your arm fully extended.
  • Keep your elbow close to your head and your palm facing inward.
  • Engage your core to maintain stability throughout the movement.

Execution

  • Lower the Weight: Inhale and slowly bend your elbow to lower the dumbbell behind your head.
  • Stretch the Triceps: Lower until you feel a good stretch in the triceps muscle.
  • Extend the Arm: Exhale and straighten your arm back to the starting position, squeezing the triceps at the top.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Elbow Stationary: Avoid letting your elbow flare out; keep it pointed forward.
  • Controlled Motion: Perform the movement slowly to avoid momentum and maximize muscle engagement.
  • Engage Core: Maintain core stability to protect your back.
  • Full Range: Use a full range of motion for maximum effectiveness.

Benefits of Single Arm Triceps Extension

  • Improves Muscle Imbalance: Allows focus on one arm at a time to correct imbalances.
  • Isolation Exercise: Effectively targets the triceps muscle.
  • Versatile: Can be performed seated or standing with dumbbells or cables.
  • Enhances Strength and Tone: Builds strength and definition in the triceps.
