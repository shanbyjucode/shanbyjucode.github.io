Incline Machine Press


Setup

  • Adjust the seat height so that the handles are at chest level when you sit down.
  • Sit with your back firmly against the pad and feet flat on the floor.
  • Grasp the handles with a firm grip, keeping your elbows slightly bent.
  • Engage your core and keep your chest lifted.

Execution

  • Press: Push the handles upward and slightly forward until your arms are fully extended but not locked.
  • Pause: Briefly squeeze your chest muscles at the top of the movement.
  • Return: Slowly lower the handles back to the starting position with control.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Maintain control: Avoid using momentum to move the weight.
  • Keep shoulders down: Prevent shrugging to avoid shoulder strain.
  • Full range of motion: Lower the handles fully without locking elbows to maximize muscle activation.
  • Breath rhythm: Exhale during pressing, inhale while lowering.

Benefits of Incline Machine Press

  • Upper Chest Focus: Targets the clavicular portion of the pectoralis major.
  • Stability: Machine guides the movement for safer and more controlled lifts.
  • Isolation: Reduces the involvement of stabilizer muscles for better chest activation.
  • Beginner Friendly: Easier to learn than free weight incline presses.
