Seated Calf


Setup

  • Sit on the calf raise machine and adjust the thigh pad so it rests securely on your thighs.
  • Place the balls of your feet on the platform with your heels hanging off.
  • Ensure your knees are bent at approximately 90 degrees and your feet are hip-width apart.
  • Release the safety lever if needed to start the movement.

Execution

  • Raise Heels: Press through the balls of your feet and lift your heels as high as possible, squeezing your calves at the top.
  • Lower Slowly: In a controlled motion, lower your heels until you feel a stretch in your calves.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Full Range of Motion: Avoid short, bouncy reps. Go all the way up and all the way down.
  • Pause and Squeeze: Hold briefly at the top to intensify the contraction.
  • Donâ€™t Rush: Keep the motion slow and controlled to prevent using momentum.
  • Foot Placement: Adjust foot positioning to target different parts of the calf muscle (closer = outer calf, wider = inner calf).

Benefits of Seated Calf Raise

  • Isolates the Soleus Muscle: Especially effective for targeting the deeper muscle of the lower leg.
  • Improves Lower Leg Strength: Essential for sprinting, jumping, and stability in compound lifts.
  • Joint Stability: Strengthens ankle joints, reducing risk of injury.
  • Muscle Definition: Helps create a balanced and defined lower body appearance.
