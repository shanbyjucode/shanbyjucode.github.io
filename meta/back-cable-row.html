Cable Row


Setup

  • Attach a straight bar or rope handle to a low pulley on a cable machine.
  • Stand facing the machine with feet shoulder-width apart and knees slightly bent.
  • Grasp the handle with both hands using an overhand or neutral grip.
  • Take a step back to create tension in the cable and stand tall with arms extended in front of you.

Execution

  • Pull: Drive your elbows straight back, pulling the handle toward your lower ribs.
  • Squeeze: Contract your shoulder blades together at the peak of the movement.
  • Return: Slowly extend your arms forward, keeping control of the weight.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Stable Stance: Keep your feet planted firmly to maintain balance.
  • Engage Core: Activate your core throughout to avoid leaning backward.
  • Focus on Form: Don’t use momentum—keep the motion slow and deliberate.

Benefits of Standing Cable Row

  • Postural Strength: Strengthens mid-back and improves posture.
  • Functional Movement: Mimics real-life pulling actions in a standing position.
  • Core Activation: Standing version recruits more core muscles than seated rows.
