Rope Extension


Setup

  • Attach a rope handle to the high pulley of a cable machine.
  • Stand facing the machine with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Grab the rope with both hands, palms facing inward (neutral grip).
  • Pull the rope down so your elbows are by your sides and forearms are parallel to the floor.
  • Engage your core and keep your chest up with a neutral spine.

Execution

  • Extend: Push the rope down by straightening your arms until fully extended at the bottom.
  • Spread the Rope: At the bottom, separate the rope ends to emphasize the triceps contraction.
  • Return: Slowly bring the rope back to the starting position, resisting the weight.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Elbows Locked: Your upper arms should remain still throughout the movement.
  • Control Each Rep: Avoid jerky motions or leaning into the pressâ€”focus on muscle engagement.
  • Full Extension: Squeeze the triceps at the bottom for maximum benefit.

Benefits of Triceps Rope Extension

  • Greater Triceps Isolation: Specifically targets the long head of the triceps.
  • Improved Arm Definition: Helps shape the back of the arms for a toned appearance.
  • Joint-Friendly: Rope allows natural wrist movement, reducing strain.
