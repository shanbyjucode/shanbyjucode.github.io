Up right row


Setup

  • Stand upright with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Hold a barbell (or EZ bar) in front of your thighs with an overhand grip, hands spaced about shoulder-width apart.
  • Keep your arms extended and your shoulders relaxed.
  • Engage your core and maintain a neutral spine throughout the movement.
  • Keep your chest up and avoid leaning backward or forward.

Execution

  • Lift: Exhale and pull the bar straight up toward your chin, keeping it close to your body.
  • Elbows High: Lead the movement with your elbows, keeping them higher than your forearms.
  • Pause: At the top, the bar should be at upper chest or shoulder level.
  • Lower: Inhale and slowly lower the bar back to the starting position with control.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Don't Overlift: Stop the bar at shoulder height—going higher can stress your shoulder joints.
  • Grip Width: Use a wider grip to place more emphasis on the shoulders and reduce wrist strain.
  • Avoid Swinging: Use controlled movements; don’t use momentum to lift the bar.
  • Neutral Wrists: Keep wrists straight and aligned with your forearms to prevent strain.

Benefits of Upright Row

  • Shoulder Development: Targets the deltoid muscles, especially the lateral head, for broader shoulders.
  • Trapezius Activation: Engages the upper traps for improved upper back strength and posture.
  • Compound Movement: Works multiple muscle groups in one motion.
  • Good for Hypertrophy: Ideal for building size and definition in the shoulders and upper back.
