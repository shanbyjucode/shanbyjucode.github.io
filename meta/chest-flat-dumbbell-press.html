Flat Dumbbell Press


Setup

  • Lie flat on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand, resting on your thighs.
  • Use your thighs to help lift the dumbbells as you lie back.
  • Position the dumbbells at chest level with your palms facing forward.
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor and your back firmly pressed against the bench.

Execution

  • Press: Push the dumbbells upward by extending your arms fully without locking the elbows.
  • Squeeze: At the top of the movement, squeeze your chest muscles briefly.
  • Lower: Slowly bring the dumbbells back down to the starting position at chest level.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Controlled Motion: Avoid using momentum; focus on slow and controlled reps.
  • Full Range: Lower the dumbbells until they are level with your chest for maximum stretch.
  • Wrist Alignment: Keep wrists straight and aligned with your forearms to prevent injury.

Benefits of Flat Dumbbell Press

  • Chest Development: Targets the pectoralis major, enhancing chest size and strength.
  • Stabilizer Engagement: Requires balance and coordination, working smaller supporting muscles.
  • Better Range of Motion: Compared to barbell presses, dumbbells allow for a deeper stretch.
