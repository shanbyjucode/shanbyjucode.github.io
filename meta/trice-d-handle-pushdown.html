D Handle Push Down


Setup

  • Attach a single D-handle to the high pulley on a cable machine.
  • Stand facing the machine with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Grab the D-handle with an overhand grip using one hand.
  • Keep your elbow close to your body and forearm parallel to the floor.
  • Brace your core and keep your chest up with a neutral spine.

Execution

  • Push Down: Extend your arm downward by straightening the elbow until your arm is fully extended.
  • Pause: Briefly pause at the bottom and contract the triceps.
  • Return: Slowly bring the handle back to the starting position without letting your elbow drift forward.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps, then switch arms.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Elbows Stationary: Only your forearm should move to isolate the triceps.
  • Avoid Using Momentum: Don’t swing your body—keep it stable.
  • Full Range of Motion: Fully extend and control the return for maximum benefit.

Benefits of D Handle Push Down

  • Unilateral Focus: Trains each arm independently to correct imbalances.
  • Better Muscle Engagement: Improved range and isolation compared to straight bar pushdowns.
  • Triceps Definition: Excellent finisher for shaping and defining the back of the arm.
