Barbell Squats


Setup

  • Position a barbell at shoulder height on a squat rack and load it with your desired weight.
  • Step under the bar so it rests across your upper traps (not your neck).
  • Grip the bar with both hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Stand tall, brace your core, and lift the bar off the rack by extending your legs.
  • Take 1â€“2 steps back and position your feet shoulder-width apart with toes slightly pointed outward.

Execution

  • Descend: Inhale and lower your hips back and down as if sitting into a chair.
  • Depth: Keep lowering until your thighs are at least parallel to the ground, or deeper if comfortable.
  • Drive Up: Exhale and press through your heels to return to standing position.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions with control and stability.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Neutral Spine: Keep your back flat and chest up throughout the movement.
  • Knees Out: Prevent your knees from caving inward by keeping them aligned with your toes.
  • Heels Grounded: Always keep your weight balanced over your heels, not your toes.
  • Depth Control: Go as low as your mobility safely allows while maintaining good form.

Benefits of Barbell Squats

  • Total Leg Development: Targets quads, hamstrings, and glutes effectively.
  • Strength & Power: Builds foundational lower body strength for all athletes.
  • Core Engagement: Strengthens the core through stabilization during the lift.
  • Functional Fitness: Improves performance in everyday movements and sports.
