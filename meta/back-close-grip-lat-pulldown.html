Close Grip Lat Pulldown


Setup

  • Attach a V-bar or close-grip handle to the high pulley of a lat pulldown machine.
  • Sit down on the machine with your thighs secured under the pads and feet flat on the floor.
  • Reach up and grasp the handle with a neutral grip (palms facing each other), hands close together.
  • Slightly lean your torso back while keeping your chest up and core engaged.

Execution

  • Pull Down: Exhale and pull the handle down toward your upper chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the bottom of the movement.
  • Pause: Hold for a brief second at the bottom for maximum contraction.
  • Controlled Return: Inhale as you slowly allow the handle to return to the starting position, fully stretching your lats.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Maintain Posture: Avoid excessive leaning; keep your spine neutral and chest lifted.
  • Full Range of Motion: Let your arms fully extend at the top for a complete stretch.
  • Use a Controlled Tempo: Avoid jerking the weight; prioritize control over momentum.
  • Focus on the Lats: Initiate the movement by pulling with your back, not your arms.

Benefits of Close Grip Lat Pulldown

  • Lat Emphasis: Targets the lower and middle parts of the latissimus dorsi more directly.
  • Biceps Engagement: Secondary activation of biceps due to the close grip.
  • Improved Back Thickness: Helps develop a dense, muscular back appearance.
  • Safe & Stable: Supported, seated position reduces risk of injury.
