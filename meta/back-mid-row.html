Mid Row


Setup

  • Sit down at a seated row machine and place your feet on the foot platform with a slight bend in your knees.
  • Grab the handle (V-bar or straight bar) with both hands, arms fully extended.
  • Keep your chest up, spine neutral, and shoulders relaxed.
  • Sit upright with a slight lean forward at the hips, keeping your core engaged.
  • Make sure your back stays straight throughout the movement—avoid rounding it.

Execution

  • Pull: Exhale and pull the handle toward your torso while keeping elbows close to your body.
  • Squeeze: Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement.
  • Return: Inhale and slowly extend your arms to return to the starting position in a controlled manner.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Maintain Posture: Keep your back straight and chest up to avoid lower back strain.
  • Don't Overextend: Do not lean too far back while pulling — stay controlled and upright.
  • Elbows Close: Keep elbows tucked in to target your mid-back muscles effectively.
  • Engage Core: Tighten your core for balance and better form throughout the rep.

Benefits of Back Mid Row

  • Back Thickness: Builds density and strength in the middle back.
  • Posture Support: Strengthens muscles responsible for maintaining proper posture.
  • Balanced Development: Complements lat exercises by targeting the rhomboids and traps.
  • Functional Strength: Improves pulling power and back stability for other lifts.
