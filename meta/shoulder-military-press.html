Military Press


Setup

  • Stand or sit with a dumbbell or barbell held at shoulder height, palms facing forward.
  • Keep your back straight and feet shoulder-width apart for stability.
  • Engage your core and keep your elbows slightly in front of your body.

Execution

  • Press: Exhale as you press the weight overhead until your arms are fully extended.
  • Lower: Inhale as you slowly lower the weight back to shoulder height with control.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of reps with steady form.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Wrists Neutral: Avoid bending your wrists to reduce strain.
  • Control the Movement: Avoid using momentum; lift with your shoulders.
  • Breath Control: Exhale while pushing up and inhale while lowering down.
  • Engage Core: Maintain a tight core to support your spine.

Benefits of Shoulder Military Press

  • Deltoid Development: Builds strength and size in all three deltoid heads.
  • Improved Shoulder Stability: Enhances overall shoulder joint stability and mobility.
  • Upper Body Strength: Increases pressing power for other lifts and daily activities.
  • Posture Improvement: Strengthens muscles that support proper posture.
