Glute Kick


Setup

  • Attach an ankle strap to a low pulley cable machine and secure it around your ankle.
  • Stand facing the machine with a slight forward lean and hold onto the machine for support.
  • Keep your working leg slightly bent and core engaged.

Execution

  • Kick Back: Exhale and drive your strapped leg back and upward in an arc motion, squeezing the glutes at the top.
  • Controlled Return: Slowly bring the leg back to the starting position without letting the weights slam.
  • Repeat: Perform all reps on one leg before switching to the other.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Isolate the Glutes: Avoid arching your back or swinging your leg to ensure you're targeting the glute muscles.
  • Engage Core: Keep your abs tight for better balance and support.

Benefits of Glute Kick

  • Glute Development: Specifically targets the gluteus maximus for toning and shaping.
  • Improves Hip Mobility: Strengthens the hip extensors and enhances range of motion.
  • Supports Lower Back: Strengthening glutes can help stabilize the lower back and pelvis.
