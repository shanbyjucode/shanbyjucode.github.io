Good Morning Bend


Setup

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and a barbell resting across your upper back (not on the neck).
  • Engage your core and maintain a slight bend in your knees.
  • Keep your chest lifted and back straight.
  • Grip the bar firmly with your hands just outside shoulder-width.

Execution

  • Hinge: Slowly hinge at the hips, pushing them backward while maintaining a straight spine.
  • Lower: Continue lowering your torso until itâ€™s nearly parallel to the floor or until you feel a strong stretch in the hamstrings.
  • Return: Reverse the motion by engaging your glutes and hamstrings to lift your torso back up.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions with control and focus.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Controlled Movement: Avoid jerking; move slowly to protect your lower back.
  • Back Alignment: Keep your spine neutral throughout the movement.
  • Depth: Only lower as far as flexibility and form allow.
  • Engage Core: Bracing your abs will support your spine during the movement.

Benefits of Good Morning Bend

  • Hamstring Strength: Effectively targets and strengthens the hamstrings.
  • Lower Back Development: Builds endurance and strength in the spinal erectors.
  • Glute Activation: Engages the glutes through the hip hinge motion.
  • Improved Posture: Promotes better spinal alignment and posture.
