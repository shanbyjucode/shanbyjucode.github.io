Abduction


Setup

  • Sit on the leg abduction machine and adjust the pads so they rest comfortably on the outside of your knees.
  • Place your feet flat on the footrests and sit upright with your back firmly against the pad.
  • Select an appropriate resistance level using the weight stack pin.
  • Hold the handles at your sides or rest your hands on your thighs for stability.

Execution

  • Push Outward: Press your legs outward against the pads, spreading them apart as far as comfortably possible.
  • Hold Briefly: Pause at the widest point and squeeze your glutes.
  • Return Slowly: Bring your legs back together in a slow, controlled motion without letting the weights slam.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Controlled Movements: Avoid using momentum. Focus on muscle contraction during both phases.
  • Back Support: Keep your back against the pad to isolate the glutes and outer thighs effectively.
  • Breathing: Exhale as you press outward, inhale as you return to start.
  • Range of Motion: Only go as wide as your hips comfortably allow without pain.

Benefits of Leg Abduction

  • Targets the Gluteus Medius: Helps strengthen the outer glutes, improving hip stability.
  • Injury Prevention: Supports healthy knee alignment and prevents hip/knee pain.
  • Enhanced Athletic Performance: Boosts lateral movement and agility.
  • Improves Aesthetics: Helps tone the outer thighs and sculpt the hip area.
