Vertical Knee Raise


Setup

  • Stand between the arms of a vertical knee raise (VKRs) station.
  • Place your forearms on the pads and grip the handles firmly.
  • Press your back against the back pad for support.
  • Let your legs hang straight down and keep your core engaged.
  • Keep your shoulders relaxed and maintain a neutral spine.

Execution

  • Lift: Slowly raise your knees toward your chest by flexing your hips.
  • Pause: Briefly squeeze your abs at the top of the movement.
  • Lower: Slowly lower your legs back to the starting position without swinging.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Control Your Movement: Avoid using momentum or swinging your legs.
  • Focus on Core Engagement: Keep your abs tight throughout the motion.
  • Keep Legs Together: Maintain control by not flaring your legs outward.

Benefits of Vertical Knee Raise

  • Core Strength: Targets the lower abdominal muscles effectively.
  • Spinal Stability: Enhances control of the hip flexors and spine.
  • Minimal Equipment: Can be done on most VKR stations in a gym.
