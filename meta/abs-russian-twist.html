Russian Twist


Setup

  • Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat (or slightly lifted for more difficulty).
  • Lean back slightly to engage your core, keeping your spine straight.
  • Clasp your hands together or hold a weight/medicine ball in front of your chest.
  • Keep your core tight and shoulders relaxed, avoiding any rounding of the back.
  • Lift your feet off the ground slightly if you want to make the exercise more challenging.

Execution

  • Twist: Rotate your torso to the right, bringing your hands or the weight beside your hip.
  • Return: Bring your hands back to center without resting.
  • Twist Opposite: Rotate to the left side, completing one rep.
  • Repeat: Continue alternating sides in a controlled, rhythmic motion.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Core Engaged: Focus on rotating from the torso, not just moving your arms.
  • Control the Movement: Avoid rushing; slow, deliberate twists are more effective.
  • Spine Neutral: Avoid rounding your backâ€”keep your posture tight and upright.
  • Breathe: Exhale as you twist, inhale as you return to center.

Benefits of Russian Twist

  • Core Strength: Targets the obliques and entire core musculature.
  • Improved Balance: Challenges stability, especially when feet are elevated.
  • Functional Rotation: Trains the body for rotational movement and control.
  • Minimal Equipment: Can be done with bodyweight, dumbbells, or medicine balls.
