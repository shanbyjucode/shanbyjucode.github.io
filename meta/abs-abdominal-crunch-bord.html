Abdominal Crunch Board


Setup

  • Lie down on the abdominal crunch board with your feet secured under the footpads.
  • Place your hands behind your head or crossed over your chest—whichever is more comfortable.
  • Bend your knees and keep your lower back in contact with the board.
  • Engage your core before starting the movement.

Execution

  • Crunch Up: Exhale and contract your abs to lift your shoulders off the board.
  • Squeeze: Pause briefly at the top of the movement to feel the abdominal contraction.
  • Controlled Return: Inhale as you slowly lower back down to the starting position without relaxing your core completely.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Don’t Pull on Your Neck: Keep tension in the core, not in your hands or neck.
  • Small, Controlled Movement: Avoid coming up too far—just enough to engage your abs.
  • Consistent Tension: Keep your abs engaged throughout the set.
  • Breathe Properly: Exhale on the crunch up, inhale on the return down.

Benefits of Abdominal Crunch Board

  • Focused Core Engagement: Isolates the abdominal muscles more effectively than flat-floor crunches.
  • Stability and Form: The board helps you maintain a consistent angle and form.
  • Variable Intensity: You can adjust incline or resistance for progression.
  • Stronger Core: Builds foundational strength and improves posture.
