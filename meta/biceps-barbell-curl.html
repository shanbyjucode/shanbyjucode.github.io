Barbell Curl


Setup

  • Stand upright with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Hold a barbell with an underhand (supinated) grip, hands about shoulder-width apart.
  • Keep your elbows close to your torso and arms fully extended.
  • Maintain a straight back and engage your core.

Execution

  • Curl the Bar: Exhale and lift the barbell toward your shoulders by bending your elbows.
  • Pause and Squeeze: Briefly hold the contraction at the top and squeeze your biceps.
  • Lower Slowly: Inhale and slowly lower the bar back to the starting position with control.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Avoid Swinging: Don’t use momentum—keep your torso still and isolate the biceps.
  • Keep Elbows Stable: Avoid moving your elbows forward or outward during the curl.
  • Use Full Range: Fully extend and contract your arms on each rep for maximum effect.
  • Choose Proper Weight: Use a weight that allows good form throughout the set.

Benefits of Barbell Curl

  • Mass Builder: One of the best exercises for adding size to the biceps.
  • Strength Gains: Helps improve your pulling strength and arm power.
  • Easy to Progress: Simple to increase resistance as strength improves.
  • Arm Definition: Isolates and sculpts the biceps for better shape and tone.
