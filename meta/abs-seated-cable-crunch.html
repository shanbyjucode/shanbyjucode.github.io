Seated Cable Crunch


Setup

  • Attach a rope handle to a high pulley on a cable machine.
  • Sit on a bench or kneeling pad facing the machine, gripping the rope with both hands.
  • Position your hands near the sides of your head, with elbows bent and close to your head.
  • Keep your spine neutral and core tight before beginning the movement.

Execution

  • Crunch Down: Exhale and contract your abs to bring your elbows toward your thighs.
  • Pause: Hold the crunch at the bottom for a moment to maximize tension.
  • Return Slowly: Inhale and slowly return to the starting position with control.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Full Range of Motion: Ensure you're curling your spine, not just bending at the hips.
  • Control the Weight: Avoid using momentum; keep the motion steady.

Benefits of Seated Cable Crunch

  • Targeted Core Activation: Isolates the rectus abdominis (six-pack) muscle effectively.
  • Adjustable Resistance: Easily modify weight to match your strength level.
  • Improved Form: More control and support than bodyweight crunches.
  • Stronger Core & Posture: Enhances balance and reduces injury risk.
