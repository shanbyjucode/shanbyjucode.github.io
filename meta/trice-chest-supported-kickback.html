Chest Supported Kickback


Setup

  • Set an incline bench to about 30–45 degrees.
  • Hold a dumbbell in one hand with a neutral grip (palm facing your body).
  • Lie chest-down on the bench, letting your arm hang straight down.
  • Keep your upper arm parallel to your torso and bend the elbow to a 90-degree angle.
  • Engage your core and keep your head neutral with your spine.

Execution

  • Kick Back: Extend your elbow to push the dumbbell straight back until your arm is fully extended.
  • Pause: Hold briefly at the top and contract your triceps.
  • Return: Slowly bring the dumbbell back to the starting position under control.
  • Repeat for the desired reps, then switch arms if needed.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Upper Arm Steady: Avoid swinging—only your forearm should move.
  • Use Light to Moderate Weight: Focus on perfect form and full contraction.
  • Chest Support: Supporting your chest minimizes body momentum and isolates the triceps.

Benefits of Chest Supported Kickback

  • Triceps Isolation: Focuses entirely on the triceps without help from the shoulders or back.
  • Controlled Movement: Bench support enforces proper form.
  • Great for Finishing Sets: Excellent burnout or finishing move for arm day.
