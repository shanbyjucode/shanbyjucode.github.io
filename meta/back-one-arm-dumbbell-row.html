One Arm Dumbbell Row


Setup

  • Place a dumbbell next to a flat bench.
  • Kneel with your right knee and place your right hand on the bench for support.
  • Keep your back flat and your core tight, forming a straight line from head to hips.
  • With your left hand, grab the dumbbell using a neutral grip (palm facing in).

Execution

  • Row the Dumbbell: Exhale and pull the dumbbell toward your hip, keeping your elbow close to your body.
  • Squeeze: Contract your lats at the top of the movement, then pause briefly.
  • Lower with Control: Inhale and slowly lower the dumbbell to the starting position without letting your shoulder drop.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep Hips Square: Avoid rotating your torso; keep your body stable and hips aligned.
  • Full Range of Motion: Stretch fully at the bottom and contract completely at the top.
  • Focus on the Back: Visualize your elbow driving the movement, not your hand.
  • Maintain Core Tension: Keep your abs engaged to support your lower back.

Benefits of One-Arm Dumbbell Row

  • Unilateral Strength: Corrects muscle imbalances between your left and right sides.
  • Improved Posture: Strengthens the upper and middle back, enhancing posture.
  • Core Engagement: Challenges your core stability due to the unilateral load.
  • Functional Strength: Mimics real-life pulling movements and builds overall back thickness.
