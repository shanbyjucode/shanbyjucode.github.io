Deadlift


Setup

  • Stand with your feet hip-width apart and your mid-foot under the barbell.
  • Bend at the hips and knees to grip the bar with both hands, just outside your knees.
  • Keep your chest up, back flat, and shoulders slightly in front of the bar.
  • Engage your core and pull your shoulder blades back and down.
  • Keep your head in a neutral position, looking a few feet in front of you.

Execution

  • Lift: Drive through your heels and extend your hips and knees simultaneously to lift the bar.
  • Stand Tall: Keep the bar close to your body as you straighten up, fully locking out your hips at the top.
  • Lower: Reverse the movement by pushing your hips back first, then bending your knees to lower the bar in a controlled way.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Keep the Bar Close: Keep the barbell close to your shins and thighs throughout the lift to maintain balance and proper form.
  • Neutral Spine: Never round your back — maintain a neutral spine from start to finish.
  • Use Proper Footwear: Wear flat shoes or deadlift barefoot for better stability.
  • Engage Core: Brace your core before lifting to protect your lower back.

Benefits of Deadlift

  • Full Body Strength: Builds strength in the back, glutes, hamstrings, and core.
  • Functional Power: Enhances overall power and athletic performance.
  • Posture Improvement: Strengthens the muscles responsible for maintaining good posture.
  • Calorie Burner: Burns high calories due to engagement of multiple large muscle groups.
