Double Arm Extension


Setup

  • Stand or sit on a bench with back support.
  • Grasp a dumbbell with both hands, holding it by one end (the inner plate or handle).
  • Raise the dumbbell overhead with your arms fully extended.
  • Keep your elbows close to your ears and your core engaged for stability.

Execution

  • Lower the Weight: Inhale and slowly lower the dumbbell behind your head by bending your elbows.
  • Stretch Fully: Lower until you feel a stretch in the triceps, keeping your elbows in place.
  • Extend and Squeeze: Exhale and extend your arms to return the dumbbell overhead, squeezing your triceps at the top.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Elbows In: Avoid flaring your elbows; keep them pointed forward to target the triceps.
  • Full Range of Motion: Use a deep stretch and full extension to maximize engagement.
  • Controlled Movement: Move slowly and avoid using momentum or arching your back.
  • Support Your Core: Keep abs tight to protect your lower back, especially when standing.

Benefits of Double Arm Triceps Extension

  • Long Head Emphasis: Targets the long head of the triceps effectively.
  • Improves Overhead Strength: Builds pressing strength useful in other lifts.
  • Great Isolation: Minimal involvement of other muscles, focusing on triceps.
  • Adaptable: Can be done seated or standing with dumbbell, barbell, or cable.
