Bar Front Raise


Setup

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart for balance and stability.
  • Hold a straight barbell or EZ bar with an overhand grip (palms facing down), hands shoulder-width apart.
  • Let the bar rest in front of your thighs, arms fully extended but elbows slightly soft.
  • Engage your core, keep your back straight, and maintain a slight bend in the knees.

Execution

  • Lift: Raise the bar in front of you with straight arms until it reaches shoulder level, keeping elbows slightly bent.
  • Pause: Hold briefly at the top to engage the front delts.
  • Lower: Slowly bring the bar back down to the starting position in a controlled manner.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions, focusing on form and control.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Grip: Use a grip that’s not too wide or too narrow to avoid unnecessary shoulder strain.
  • Movement: Avoid swinging or using momentum—control the lift through the delts.
  • Breathing: Exhale while lifting, inhale as you lower the bar.
  • Posture: Keep your chest up and shoulders relaxed, don’t arch your lower back.

Benefits of Bar Front Raise

  • Targets the anterior deltoids for front shoulder development.
  • Improves shoulder aesthetics and symmetry.
  • Enhances upper-body posture and lifting mechanics.
  • Helps with performance in pressing and overhead movements.
