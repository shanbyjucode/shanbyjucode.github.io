Barbell Row


Setup

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip a barbell with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and chest up.
  • Let the bar hang at armâ€™s length just below your knees, with your arms fully extended.
  • Engage your core and maintain a flat back throughout the movement.

Execution

  • Pull: Drive your elbows back and row the barbell toward your lower chest or upper abs.
  • Squeeze: Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement.
  • Lower: Slowly lower the bar back to the starting position in a controlled manner.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Neutral Spine: Keep your head in line with your spine to avoid strain.
  • Elbows Close: Pull with your elbows and keep them close to your body to better target the lats.
  • No Jerking: Avoid using momentum; focus on a smooth, controlled motion.

Benefits of Barbell Row

  • Total Back Development: Targets upper, middle, and lower back muscles.
  • Improved Posture: Strengthens the posterior chain, helping posture and spinal alignment.
  • Grip Strength: Helps improve forearm and grip strength due to holding the heavy barbell.
