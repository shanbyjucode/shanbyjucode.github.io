Chest Supported Straight Arm pulldown


Setup

  • Set an adjustable bench at about a 30â€“45 degree incline and place it facing the cable machine.
  • Attach a straight bar or EZ bar to the high pulley.
  • Lie face down on the bench with your chest supported and feet anchored on the ground.
  • Grab the bar with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, arms fully extended.
  • Keep your head neutral and core engaged throughout the movement.

Execution

  • Pull: In a controlled motion, bring the bar down in an arc path until it reaches your hips or just past them.
  • Squeeze: At the bottom, squeeze your lats without bending the arms too much.
  • Return: Slowly allow the bar to rise back to the starting position, keeping tension in the lats.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Minimal Arm Movement: Keep your arms mostly straight with a slight bend at the elbows.
  • Engage Lats: Focus on pulling through the lats, not the arms or shoulders.
  • Controlled Motion: Avoid jerky movements; maintain a slow and steady pace.

Benefits of Incline Laying Straight Arm Pulldown

  • Strict Isolation: Laying down prevents momentum and isolates the lats more effectively.
  • Back Width Focus: Targets the upper and outer lats to enhance back width.
  • Improved Form: The incline bench supports better posture and limits cheating.
