Close Grip Bench Press


Setup

  • Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the floor.
  • Grip the barbell with your hands positioned shoulder-width apart or slightly closer.
  • Unrack the bar and hold it directly above your chest with arms fully extended.
  • Engage your core and maintain a slight arch in your lower back throughout the lift.

Execution

  • Lower: Slowly bring the barbell down toward the lower part of your chest while keeping your elbows tucked in close to your body.
  • Pause: Briefly pause just above your chest to maintain control and tension.
  • Press: Push the bar back up by driving through your triceps and chest until your arms are fully extended.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions with controlled movement.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Elbow Position: Keep your elbows close to your sides to maximize triceps activation and reduce shoulder strain.
  • Grip: Don’t go too narrow — shoulder-width grip is ideal to avoid wrist or shoulder discomfort.
  • Control: Avoid bouncing the bar off your chest. Use slow, controlled reps.
  • Breathing: Inhale as you lower the bar, exhale as you press up.

Benefits of Close Grip Bench Press

  • Targets the triceps more than the standard bench press.
  • Improves pressing strength and lockout power.
  • Engages the inner chest and front deltoids.
  • Useful accessory movement for improving overall bench press performance.
