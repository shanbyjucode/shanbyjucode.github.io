Flat Bench Press


Setup

  • Lie flat on a bench with your eyes directly under the barbell.
  • Plant your feet firmly on the ground for stability.
  • Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width using an overhand grip.
  • Keep your shoulder blades pulled back and chest up.
  • Unrack the bar carefully and position it above your chest with arms extended.

Execution

  • Lower the Bar: Inhale and lower the bar slowly to the mid-chest area, keeping elbows at about a 45-degree angle.
  • Press Up: Exhale and push the bar back up by extending your arms fully, keeping your wrists straight.
  • Control: Maintain control throughout the movement to avoid bouncing the bar off your chest.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Full Range of Motion: Lower the bar all the way to your chest without bouncing.
  • Keep Core Tight: Engage your abs and avoid arching your lower back excessively.

  • Spotter Help: Use a spotter for safety, especially with heavier weights.

Benefits of Flat Bench Press

  • Builds Upper Body Strength: Primarily targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.
  • Improves Push Power: Enhances overall pressing strength and power.
  • Compound Exercise: Engages multiple muscle groups for efficiency.
  • Foundation for Progression: A key lift for building muscle mass and upper body size.
