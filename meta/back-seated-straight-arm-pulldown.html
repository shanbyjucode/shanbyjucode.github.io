Back Supported Straight Arm Pulldown


Setup

  • Attach a straight bar to the high pulley on a cable machine.
  • Set up a bench or seat in front of the cable machine and sit upright.
  • Grab the bar with a shoulder-width overhand grip (palms facing down).
  • Extend your arms straight in front of you, slightly above shoulder level, keeping a slight bend in the elbows.
  • Engage your core and keep your chest lifted throughout.

Execution

  • Pull: With arms extended, pull the bar down in an arc motion until your hands reach your thighs.
  • Squeeze: Contract your lats at the bottom of the movement.
  • Return: Slowly raise the bar back to the starting position with control.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Effectiveness

  • Maintain Arm Position: Keep arms nearly straight with only a slight bend in the elbows throughout.
  • Don't Use Momentum: Focus on using your lats rather than swinging or leaning.
  • Full Stretch: Let your lats fully stretch at the top without losing form.

Benefits of Seated Straight Arm Pulldown

  • Lat Isolation: Specifically targets the latissimus dorsi without involving the biceps.
  • Improves Mind-Muscle Connection: Great for focusing on lat engagement.
  • Enhances Back Width: Helps develop the V-taper appearance by building outer lats.
